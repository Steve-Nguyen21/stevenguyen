About

Introduction:

Hello, I'm Steve Nguyen!

I'm currently a student at George Mason University majoring in Information Technology with a concentration in Cyber Security. I've always had a passion for computers and technology in general.
I hope to eventually advance into a blue team related role in the future after gaining enough experience.

How did I get interested in Cyber Security?

On my second semester at Mason, I took a introduction class called: Information Security Fundamentals and was intrigued by the different types attacks used by threat actors and APTs.
I thought to myself, how do I keep myself and others secure in this day and age of technology?

That curiousity made me install Linux on a Virtual Machine. I first started off by learning a bit about the Linux Command line by playing a game called Overthewire: Bandit. I also joined a club called Mason Competitive Cyber to learn from others with similar hobbies.

What projects have I worked on?

Webscraper

    Brief Overview:

  • This webscraper fetches prices of Graphic Cards and Hard Drives, depending on which you want to search up from New-egg. It can be both. It'll then create a file and save the data into a csv(comma seperated values) file.
  • It proceeds to ask you if you want to email the prices to someone in-case you would like to share the price to a friend.


    • I made this to help me learn about webscraping and smtp libraries to apply my Python knowledge. I was also looking to upgrade my graphics card at the time.

Keylogger/Keymonitoring

    Brief Overview:

  • This script listen to keys typed by the user and gives you a timestamp on when it's pressed along with what they typed individually.
  • It scans the system to give you information on what the victim's private and public ip, broadcast ip, partitions, hardware, etc.
  • Sends you the files created via email you provide in the code.


    • This was made for educational purposes to learn how keyloggers work and how to prevent them.
